Referee Guidelines

  1. For each contributed paper and poster assigned, you are asked to assess the work based on the following four criteria:

    • Originality
    • Significance
    • Contribution to Theory and/or Methodology
    • Organization, Style, and Clarity of Presentation

  2. For each of the four criteria, you are asked to assign one of the following ratings from the drop-down menu:

    • Excellent
    • Good
    • Fair
    • Poor
    • Very Poor

  3. You are then asked to provide an overall assessment by selecting from the following recommendations from the drop-down menu:

    • Accept
    • Accept with a few minor revisions
    • Reject

  4. For both "Referee's Expertise on the Topic" and "Amount of Rewriting Required", you are asked to assign one of the following values using the radio buttons provided:

    • Low
    • Medium
    • High

    The rating of referee expertise by the referee is used to provide a weighted value.

  5. For the purposes of assisting the authors in understanding your assessments and providing additional information to the Program Committee co-chairs, you are asked to provide narrative discussions for the following:

    • Comments to the Authors (Mandatory)
      • Main Contributions
      • Positive Aspects
      • Negative Aspects
      • Further Comments
    • Confidential Comments for Committee Use Only
