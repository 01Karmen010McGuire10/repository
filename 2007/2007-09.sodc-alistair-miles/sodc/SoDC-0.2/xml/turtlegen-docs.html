2. Translation

SoDC-XML is of course a concrete syntax of RDF, and can be transformed to other concrete RDF syntaxes.

The Turtlegen stylesheet is an XSL transformation which will generate a Turtle document from an SoDC-XML documents, and is at the link below:

  • turtlegen.xsl -- Turtlegen Stylesheet

3. Examples

For example, the SoDC-XML document below...

<?xml version="1.0" encoding="UTF-8"?>
<graph xmlns="http://purl.org/net/sodc/xml">  

  <resource>
    <uri>http://example.com/book#1</uri>
    <property uri="http://purl.org/dc/elements/1.1/title">
      <value>
        <plainliteral lang="en">Winnie the Pooh</plainliteral>
      </value>
    </property>
    <property uri="http://purl.org/dc/elements/1.1/creator">
      <value>
        <uri>http://example.com/person#1</uri>
      </value>
    </property>
    <property uri="http://example.com/property#price">
      <value>
        <typedliteral datatype="http://www.w3.org/2001/XMLSchema#decimal">9.99</typedliteral>
      </value>
    </property>
  </resource>

  <resource>
    <uri>http://example.com/person#1</uri>
    <property uri="http://xmlns.com/foaf/0.1/name">
      <value>
        <plainliteral lang="en">A. A. Milne</plainliteral>
      </value>
    </property>
  </resource>
  
</graph>

...is transformed by the Turtlegen stylesheet to the Turtle document below...

<http://example.com/book#1> <http://purl.org/dc/elements/1.1/title> """Winnie the Pooh"""@en ;
   <http://purl.org/dc/elements/1.1/creator> <http://example.com/person#1> ;
   <http://example.com/property#price> """9.99"""^^<http://www.w3.org/2001/XMLSchema#decimal> .
<http://example.com/person#1> <http://xmlns.com/foaf/0.1/name> """A. A. Milne"""@en .
