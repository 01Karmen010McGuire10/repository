Introduction

The CEN/ISSS Workshop on Dublin Core Metadata for Multimedia Information - Dublin Core (MMI-DC) of the European Committee for Standardization CEN (http://www.cenorm.be/sh/mmi-dc/) organised a workshop on the subject of standardisation and co-operation issues related to interoperability between Government Information Programmes in Europe.

This invitational workshop took place in conjunction with EGOV04 (see: http://falcon.ifs.uni-linz.ac.at/news/cfp_egovernment2004.html) on 3 September 2004 in Zaragoza, Spain. The agenda for this workshop is included below.

The background of this work was that many countries in Europe are currently developing organisational and technical frameworks for the management of and access to government information. The work in CEN aims to investigate issues and propose common approaches related to management and exchange of information, with the goal to find and promote interoperable solutions on a European scale.

Invitations went out to the MMI-DC workshop list, the DC-Goverenment list, CEN membership, the eGovernment Working Group of the European Public Administration Network and personal contacts of the Project Team members.

Report of meeting

The morning session started with an overview by Anne Lehouck of the European Commission’s Directorate General Enterprise, Unit D4, of European Commission policies in the context of the eEurope standardisation action plan. She solicited input from the community in defining the strategies and activities in the future. A proposed EU Communication on the role of the EU has been recently published for public comment.

Themis Tambouris of the CEN Project Team presented the draft CWAs that were out for public comment since July 2004.

Makx Dekkers of the CEN Project Team then presented the historical background for the current CEN work as emanating from earlier activities in the European Commission’s Programme for Interchange of Documents between Administrations (IDA) and specifically the Management of Information Resourcesfor eGovernment (MIReG) that specified a model and metadata approach based on an investigation of national programmes across Europe.

Maewyn Cumming presented the outcomes of a workshop on Semantic Interoperability that was held in Norway in June. She solicited comments on the European Interoperability Framework that was recently published.

After the coffee break, several participants in the meeting presented their work in projects and national information programmes, including Sweden, Finland, the Netherlands, the UK, and Switzerland. In question-and-answer sessions after each presentation, the participants discussed issues arising from the presentations.

The afternoon session concentrated on two subjects that arose from the issues indicated by participants on the registration forms and from the discussions during the morning programme.

Governance and sustainability:

  • Is there a need/potential for a central eGovernment centre in Europe to maintain a European metadata framework?
  • Who would need to take the lead?
  • Where would the money come from?

Controlled vocabularies, terminology, maintenance

  • What terminology and modeling approaches are appropriate, what level of detail is required?
  • Can we build communities of interest for development of joint vocabularies?
  • How can controlled vocabularies be ratified?
  • How to keep governmental metadata up-to-date?

The discussion on these points led to the conclusions below.

 

Conclusions

The participants recommended that the European Commission set up or help set up a central eGovernment Interoperability Centre to support the eGovernment policy objectives, and provide longer-term funding (as opposed to project funding) for such an entity. The entity should be responsible for the maintenance of a European Metadata Framework, and in addition provide services to the community in the form of discussion platforms for feedback and experience sharing, as well as act as an Observatory for developments in the member states.

The participants recommended a CEN Work Item to investigate and identify or propose a common subset of controlled vocabulary terms (also sometimes referred to as a Government Category List).

The participants wanted to see a further eGovernment workshop like the one in Zaragoza, or a track in the European eGovernment Conference that will take place in the UK in 2005, with a specific focus on metadata quality -- this could also be a small work item for MMI-DC.
