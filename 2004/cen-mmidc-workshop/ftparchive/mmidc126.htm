Metadata Quality for Government / Makx Dekkers

Annex 2-eEurope Standardisation Action Plan

Grant Application Form

 

Part I – General Information

Title:

Metadata Quality for Government

Organisation:

CEN/CENELEC/ETSI

Short description: <please provide a short technical description of the proposed activity in maximum 15 lines>

Government Information Programmes are developing metadata frameworks and co-operate on a European level to realise interoperability across borders.

Apart from the definition and implementation of metadata frameworks enabling an interoperable and sustainable metadata environment, national programmes need to consider requirements and approaches towards ensuring metadata quality and long-term maintenance of metadata.

The need for addressing these issues is recognised by many national activities. However, there is currently little guidance or information about quality and maintenance of metadata.

Under this work item, a meeting will be organised in conjunction with the European eGovernment Conference that will take place in the UK in 2005. This meeting will bring together representatives from across Europe and possibly from other parts of the world to discuss common challenges related to metadata quality and maintenance with the objective to come to a set of recommendations for common approaches.

Output/deliverables: <please indicate the expected deliverables and the timetable for completion>

This work item will produce the following deliverable:
  1. CEN Workshop Agreement, containing:
    • Report of the meeting with links to presentations delivered at the meeting
    • Recommendations for common approaches for metadata quality and maintenance

Requested financial support: <please indicate the requested financial contribution from the EU/EFTA in absolute figures, as well as a percentage of the total eligible costs>

A total of 25 person days funded by EU/EFTA with voluntary effort of up to 50 person days from contributors and workshop participants.

 

 

Part II – Policy relevance and expected impact

Policy relevance: <please describe the relevance of the proposed initiative to the eEurope 2005 Action Plan and which specific policy objectives are supported>

This work item falls under the policy area of the eGovernment section of the eEurope 2005: information society for all policy.

Expected impact: <please describe the expected impact of the proposed standardisation initiative in support of the eEurope 2005 Action Plan. Who would benefit potentially from this initiative? What would be the cost of non-implementation?>

The key benefits of this work item are:
    1. The workshop will enable sharing of experience and establishment of contacts across government information programmes in Europe. This will contribute towards the emergence of common rules and approaches for the long-term quality and availability of metadata related to government information, enabling citizens and business across the EU to find relevant information and services through high-quality and well-maintained metadata.

 

 

 

 

Taxonomies for eGovernment / Colette Coles

Annex 2-eEurope Standardisation Action Plan

Grant Application Form

DRAFT 2004.09.15 Colette Coles

Part I – General Information

Title:

Taxonomies for eGovernment

Organisation:

CEN/CENELEC/ETSI

Short description: <please provide a short technical description of the proposed activity in maximum 15 lines>

Each EU government should be using taxonomies across their information and services resources to enable administrators to manage content and services, and citizens to locate information and services. Governments and their citizens also need to exchange information across administrative boundaries, as well as having system interoperability; semantic interoperability is an essential component that will allow this to happen.

In order to achieve semantic interoperability, Governments need to either map their taxonomies to other government taxonomies or use a common taxonomy. Each option has limitations: all using the same taxonomy is not practical yet and mapping is in practise very difficult because of the conceptual difference between different taxonomies.

Under this work item, a meeting will be organised in conjunction with the European eGovernment Conference that will take place in the UK in 2005. This meeting will bring together representatives from across Europe and possibly from other parts of the world to discuss common challenges related to semantic interoperability and issues of shared government taxonomies.

Output/deliverables: <please indicate the expected deliverables and the timetable for completion>

This work item will produce the following deliverable:
  1. CEN Workshop Agreement, containing:
    • Survey of taxonomies and their core features currently being used across governments.
    • Recommendations regarding the need for a government administrative taxonomy

Requested financial support: <please indicate the requested financial contribution from the EU/EFTA in absolute figures, as well as a percentage of the total eligible costs>

A total of 55 person days funded by EU/EFTA with voluntary effort of up to 55 person days from contributors and workshop participants.

Part II – Policy relevance and expected impact

Policy relevance: <please describe the relevance of the proposed initiative to the eEurope 2005 Action Plan and which specific policy objectives are supported>

This work item falls under several policies areas:

    1. The eGovernment section of the eEurope 2005: information society for all policy.
    2. The semantic interoperability policy in the European interoperability framework for pan-european eGovernment Services.
    3. OASIS eGov TC semantic interoperability

Expected impact: <please describe the expected impact of the proposed standardisation initiative in support of the eEurope 2005 Action Plan. Who would benefit potentially from this initiative? What would be the cost of non-implementation?>

The key benefits of this work time are:
    1. It would contribute towards governments exchanging meaningful data and enable citizens to search for concepts across EU to find relevant information and services.
    2. Promote taxonomy standards across EU, ensuring that government taxonomies conform to interoperability standards
    3. Enable governments who have yet to develop their own taxonomies to adopt established taxonomies and share best practices.
