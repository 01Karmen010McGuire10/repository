Status of this document

This document is the Final Working Draft of the Dublin Core Metadata Initiative [DCMI], and is intended to reflect consensus reached within the Date Working Group [DCDate] of the DCMI. This document reports decisions to date by the DC Date WG and is based primarily on the results of a formal polling of the WG during December, 1999 (see: http://www.mailbase.ac.uk/lists/dc-date/1999-12/0021.html for a summary of the voting results).

This is the FINAL version of the Working Draft. Publication as a Final Working Draft does not imply endorsement by the DCMI. This document has been submitted to the DCMI Usage Committee as input into the development of the Qualifiers for the Dublin Core Metadata Element Set.

Comments and feedback should be sent to the working group mailing list, dc-date@mailbase.ac.uk, the archives for which may be browsed at < http://www.mailbase.ac.uk/lists/dc-date/archive.html > (NOTE, you must be a member of the WG to post messages to the WG) or, alternatively, send your feedback to the Editor of this Working Draft.

1. Introduction

Over and above its recommendations for qualifiers, the WG has reached consensus on the following:

Work with DC Coverage:

  • DC Date should make it a priority to synchronize recommendations with the DC Coverage WG [DCCoverage]

Schemes

  • Endorse the use of W3CDTF (http://www.w3.org/TR/NOTE-datetime ) but no other schemes at this time.

Start/End technique to capture ranges:

  • Endorse expressing date ranges through the explicit enumeration of "start" and "end" qualifiers/tokens (e.g., Valid. Start and Valid.End)
  • Endorse the application of this technique:
    • To DC Date (unqualified)
    • To all approved DC Date element qualifiers
  • Use the words/tokens, "Start" and "End" to express this concept.

2. Qualifiers - endorsed

The qualifiers enumerated below are intended for the Date element of the Dublin Core Element Set [DCES].

 Qualifiers - endorsed : The following DC Date qualifiers have earned endorsement by the DC Date Working Group:

  • Created
  • Issued
  • Modified
  • Valid
  • Available
  • "periodName" (contingent on parallel adoption by the DC Coverage WG. Absent adoption by DC Coverage, DC Date does not endorse "periodName")

Created

"Date of creation of the resource."

Yes

No

The date of creation or origin of a resource is widely recognized as a noteworthy date across many communities.
