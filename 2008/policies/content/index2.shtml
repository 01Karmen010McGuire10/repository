Overview of the DCMI Web site (http://dublincore.org)

All of the pages on the website have server-side includes for the header and the footer. The home page has a bolder and bigger style of header and footer, but other than that, everything remains the same throughout the entire site. Pages that get the server side includes have the .shtml file suffix. Other files on the site that do not get server side includes (like PowerPoint presentations) should have the .html or .htm file suffix.

Below this Home Page is the first level of information.

These areas are:

  • About the Initiative
  • DCMI News
  • Documents
  • Groups
  • Meetings and Presentations
  • Projects
  • Resources
  • Tools & Software
  • Workshops

All of these pages have a left-hand column for navigation and a right-hand column for content. Depending on the type of information that was being presented, there are 2 different structures for these pages. For content that is relatively static, the right content column contains brief descriptions of the content that is available further down into the site. The intent is to give the user a quick intro to what would be available if they clicked on the "more info..." link.

Pages that have this structure include: About the Initiative and Resources. The Documents, Groups, and Meetings & Presentations sections, while they don't give brief intros to further information, do provide a list of items that pertain to that section. For all of these sections, the left-hand navigation links to different sections of the content in the right-hand column.

Sections that have dynamic information (News, Projects and Tools & Software) are in a bit of a flux right now. The intent is to have the left-hand navigation links be actual call-outs to a database with the retrieved information then showing up in the right-hand column content area. However since that service is not quite ready for prime time, there is an interim page structure.

In all cases, we stay w/ the left-hand navigation - right-hand content structure. For DCMI News and for Projects, the left-hand navigation links to separate .shtml pages. For Tools & Software, the left-hand navigation links to different parts of the right-hand column content (w/o going to separate pages).

For the entire site, once you get to the bottom level of the content, you get a plain page w/ just header & footers - no left hand navigation. This is most often used in the "About the Initiative" section.

HOME PAGE:

The DCMI Home page is divided into five sections.

  • The first section at the top of the page is the Header. This is a server- side include. Links in this section are: About the Initiative, DCMI News, Documents, Groups, Resources, Tools & Software, Meetings & Presentations and Projects.
  • Left-hand side navigation:
    • Overview Section - To a certain extent this is a repeat of the Header navigation. We added "Contact" and dropped "Groups" from the top. We felt that a repeat of the header links would give users 2 places to "expect" the information that they are looking for. We added "Contact" (raising it in visibility from under the "About the Initiative" section) on advice from Zot. Also because we felt that it might be a commonly sought link. We dropped "Groups" from the Overview section, because they are listed on the right-hand side.
    • Ready Reference Section - This is a group of no more than 6 of the top requested pages on the site. This will change once the site goes live and we can track what the most requested pages are, but for now we seeded this section w/ the pages that we thought most people would be interested in and which were a bit deeper in the site.
    • Search - This is pretty obvious.
    • Mirrors - Once our mirror sites stablize, they will be listed under a "Mirrors" banner on this side of the front page.
  • Right-hand side navigation:
    • Working Groups and Special Interest Groups - The thought was to list the individual working groups on the home page as a service to "power users." Community members who knew exactly where they wanted to go.
  • Center Column News Items: This section is very dynamic with all announcements relating to DCMI showing up here for some period of time. These announcements are listed in a news.txt file. This is part of the RSS news feed that we have on the site. All of the news items will have an icon associated with it. The different types of news announcements that we anticipate are: "General Announcements" - items that relate to DCMI as a whole; "New Document" for newly added documents or announcements when a document changes status; "New Tool & Software" for newly available tools and/or software; "New Project" for newly announced projects using Dublin Core; and "New Meeting" for announcements of upcoming meetings, seminars or calls for participation. Any links that are made from these news items to documents or pages on the site must have the complete URL - not a relative URL. This news feed will be picked up by sites outside of the DCMI site and we need to have the complete link in order for people off site to follow it. To keep scrolling at a minimum, we are trying to keep the number of news items listed to 7 or less. All of these news items are also listed in the appropriate section of DCMI News.
  • The fifth section is the server-side footer.

ABOUT THE INITIATIVE:

This section deal with areas that reflect the structure, personnel and the policies of the DCMI Initiative itself. The information in this area is relatively static. The sections in this directory are:

  • DCMI Overview: The Overview is a general introduction to DCMI, what the element set is, and how people can participate in the discussions.
  • DCMI Community: This is a listing of the types of organizations that participate in the DCMI with additional information about the institutions that have sponsored DC workshops.
  • DCMI History: This area is still under development. The intent is for anecdotal and personal information, remarks and photographs that pertain to the history of the DCMI
  • DCMI Organization: This is an indepth description of how DCMI works. It also includes pages listing:
    • DC-EC and DC-AC committee members
    • Past committee members
  • DCMI Contacts: A simple list of contacts that can be made w/ the web team, a separate link to Zot for media inquiries and places for people to submit new news items, projects, tools etc.
  • DCMI Liaisons: A listing of memoranda of understanding and agreements with other metadata and standards organizations
  • DCMI Technology: This is a section that started w/ Zot. It mostly comes across as part of a glossary, but I think it should and will cover the new technologies that are used on this web site - such as RSS, RDF, DC metadata etc.
  • DCMI Policies: A list of the policies that are in force on this web site (privacy and copyright - both of which were borrowed w/ permission from W3C) and the Public Open Source Software license.

DCMI NEWS

All the content in this section is listed in reverse chronological order, with the most recent information listed at the top. There are 5 different types of announcements that are listed in the news section. Each of these announcements is listed in a .txt page. The structure of the news item is similar to the news.txt file that populates the home page, but in the news section, we don't repeat the news icon with each item. The different types of announcements are:

  • General Announcements - news items about:
    • changes in the DCMI structure
    • changes in DCMI personnel
    • press releases
    • announcements about new liaisons and/or changes in current liaisons
    • standards news
    • web site changes
    • foreign language translations
    • new resources
    • publications by the DCMI Directorate
    • working group announcements
    • anything else that doesn't fit into the other 4 categories
    • publication of DCMI Update
  • Meetings & Presentations - news items about:
    • calls for participation for meetings (working groups and DCMI workshops)
    • announcements about workshops/conferences or other events where how to use Dublin Core is a promenient part of the proceedings
    • announcements about upcoming speeches/presentations that will be given by DCMI principals
  • New Documents - news items about:
    • announcements about any new official DCMI document - working draft, proposed recommendations, recommendations and notes.
    • announcements about changes in document status (revised w.d.; wd to prec; prec to rec etc.)
  • New Projects - news items about:
    • new projects that are using Dublin Core
    • changes in projects that are using Dublin Core
  • New Tools & Software - news items about:
    • new tools and software that help with the creation, collection etc. of Dublin Core metadata

DOCUMENTS

The DCMI web site is the official repository for all official DCMI documents. If the document is not on the site, it cannot be considered "official." This will require a culture change for the working drafts since past practice has been to allow the working group chairs to put them up where ever it was most convienent for them and then linking to them off of the DCMI web site. A decision should also be made about whether the various draft versions of past documents should be brought onto the site.

On the index page for this directory (/documents/index.shtml) all the documents are listed by publishing date and are in reverse chronological order, grouped by document type.

Documents are added onto the web site based on the date of publication. The directory structure for official documents is as follows:

Under the documents directory, there should be sub-directories showing the year of publication /month of publication/day of publication /name of file/

The name of the file is important since it is a name that should stay w/ the document throughout it's life-cycle - thus Education working group - working draft is not a good title. However a document that is looking at how best to encode qualified Dublin Core metadata in HTML could have the following file structure:

2000/08/05/dcq-html/

You just have to remember to keep it unique from all of the other previously named documents.

RESOURCES

This section is intended to act as the educational component of the DCMI web site. It currently contains a:

  • Bibliography
  • FAQ
  • a list of DCMI document translations
    • There has been some discussion that these documents belong on the official DCMI document lists. However since they do not go through an "approval" process and just show up when "translated" bya an individual or agency, I made the decision that they do not belong on the official documents page. Each of the affected documents does have a link to this page so that uses can see if there is an appropriate translation for their needs available. Once there is some sort of process to officially approve of the translations, then they can/should become part of the official document list.

Other resources that I intend(ed) to add:

  • A listing of other user guides that are available from the different projects the employ Dublin Core.
  • On-line education (distance education) modules that teach how to create Dublin Core records
  • Glossary - the usage guide created by Diane Hillmann has a glossery link, but it is to an out-of-date site that uses the old DC logo. I thought it would be better to bring that glossary onto the web site and then extend it on the DCMI web site.
  • Seminars and other conferences that are not sponsored by DCMI but that teach Dublin Core metadata

WORKSHOPS

The index page for the workshops directory (/workshops/index.shtml) should list in reverse chronological order all of the official DCMI workshops or conferences. Each listing should include the date of the workshop, location, co-sponsoring institution, and link to the workshop home page w/in the workshop directory.

Each workshop should have its own directory. An index page in the directory should contain a link to the call for participation, workshop homepage, workshop report and any additional links to other resources or meeting notes that come from the meeting.

If DCMI maintained the workshop site - all pages should go into this directory.

If the site was developed and hosted by a different institution, then once the workshop is completed, we should get a dump of the pages and place them into the ARCHIVES. Then off of the workshop page /workshops/dcX/index.shtml - we can provide a link to the official workshop web site as well as a link to the local cached version.

 

DIRECTORIES THAT ARE IMPORTANT BUT HIDDEN FROM VIEW TO MOST USERS:
