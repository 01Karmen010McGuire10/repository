Imagery

Imagery refers to any photographs, illustrations or other pieces of art that you use on Dublin Core Metadata Initiative communications. Imagery can be a powerful reflection of our identity and should consistently express the DCMI Key Message. It should be friendly, accessible, new and imaginative. Because we are a global organization, it should also be geared toward a global audience. And always think in terms of audience benefits when choosing imagery.

General guidelines

  • Be sensitive to our audiences and their cultures.
  • Use people/models that represent actual DCMI users.
  • Use illustrations to depict abstract ideas or editorialize. Avoid using clip art.
  • Concentrate on the main image, and be careful not to create visual clutter, which can detract from the message.

Image sources

Because the images you choose for any DCMI project immediately become associated with DCMI's image, our guidelines require they be of high quality and professionally produced.
