DC Title Working Group Proposal for Title Qualifier
Date: 2000-02-10

Element DC Title Comments and Scope Note

Proposed Title Qualifier

Date: 2000-02-10

Status: Working Group Proposal

Note: This document represents the current status of the discussion in the Working Group DC Title and also reflects a vote made in the Working Group. The comments and recommendations made here should be seen as a product of concensus of the Working Group members and is thus submitted to the DC Advisory Committee and DC Usage group for continued evaluation.

DC Title

Name: Title
Identifier: Title
Definition: A name given to the resource.
Comment: Typically, a title will be a name by which the resource is formally known. In library terms, the Title includes the main title. For best practice, the Title also includes subtitles unless otherwise locally determined by specific local qualifier.

Information for titles of resources which are "physically" parts of other resources (e.g. articles in journals, chapters of books or dissertations, etc.) should use the Title as the formal title of the resource being described and DC Relation for the Title and Identifier of the resource in which it is part.

Transliterated titles also use DC title as a repeatable element. These are "equal" to the title itself.

Uniform titles should be placed in DC Relation.

Proposed Qualifier for DC Title

The DC Title Working Group has thusfar endorsed one qualifier for the DC Title element:

DC Title.Alternative

Name: Title.Alternative
Identifier: Title.Alternative
Definition: Any alternative title used as a substitute or alternative to the forma title (or titles under which the resource is formally known).
Comment: This includes abbreviations for titles (whereby a scheme indication would be useful), shortened titles, translations of titles into languages other than the language of the resource (with the additional use of the language qualifier "LANG"), vernacular titles or titles be which a certain work is well-known but is not the formal title (e.g. "Schönfelder's" for the Schönfelder Commentary to German Civial Laws).

Translations of titles may be official (i.e., on the title page as a "parallel title") or inofficial (translations of the title into another language to facilitate resource discovery purposes when the title is not official part of the resource itself). For best practices, the "LANG" qualifier should be added to all translations of the formal title of a resource.

Additional Comment for Best Practice: Information regarding "Edition," "Version" or "Release" have not been included as components of the title; it is recommended that they be a separate qualifier for DC Description (see DC Description.Release).
